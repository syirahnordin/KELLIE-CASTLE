Kellie’s Castle, Perak

Photographed and Written by Faidz Zainal

For over a century, tales of rags-to-riches took place in this part of Batu Gajah district. A symbol of former British colonialist, fortunes were derived from the thriving rubber plantations and tin mining exploitation at the turn of 20th century in the state of Perak.

Today, a ruin that was once shrouded with creepers and trees has become a popular tourist attraction. The stately building, Kellie’s Castle was built in 1915 by William Kellie Smith, a Scottish planter. The castle was an extension from his existing manor, the Kellas House where he and his family lives since 1910.

Inspired by Mughal architecture, he brought in dozens of Indian craftsmen to embark on the construction. A plan was drawn to include an indoor tennis court, an elevator shaft, a rooftop courtyard for entertaining guests and maze of tunnels underneath the castle.

The difficulty of getting materials from Europe due to First World War halted the construction. The misfortune ensued as the Indian craftsmen suffered from Spanish Flu pandemic after the war. A Hindu temple, still exists to this date, was built near the site to ward off “evil spirit”. An effigy of William Smith can be found at the temple amongst the other statues of deities, as appreciation by the Indian workers.

The construction of such grandeur has never been completed. In 1926, William Smith died of pneumonia in Portugal while he was on his way to visit his family in England. The estate was sold to another party, but the new owner has no interest in developing the castle. As time goes by, the jungle engulfed the castle, hiding it from the outside world.

Parallel to the castles in Scotland, where William Smith was born, ghost stories emerged from the bricks and mortar of Kellie’s Castle. Some claimed they have seen a spirit, possibly William Smith himself, walking along the corridors. Some had seen a little girl playing in one of the rooms, while others saw a legless guard patrolling the area.

If you are in the castle now, what would you seek?
