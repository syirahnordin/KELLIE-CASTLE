The Fascinating History of Kellie’s Castle

  • ▸William Kellie Smith, a Scottish planter, arrived in Malaysia in the late 19th century and made a fortune in the rubber industry. Inspired by his love for his wife, Agnes, he decided to build a grand castle as a symbol of his affection. Construction of Kellie’s Castle began in 1915 and was intended to be William Kellie Smith’s ‘Taj Mahal’ to his beloved wife. However, tragedy struck when William Kellie Smith passed away in 1926, leaving the castle unfinished.

  • Despite its incomplete state, Kellie’s Castle stands as a testament to the ambition and vision of its creator. The castle’s construction was halted due to financial difficulties and the outbreak of World War I, which further hindered its completion. Today, Kellie’s Castle serves as a reminder of the dreams and aspirations of William Kellie Smith. It is a fascinating glimpse into the past and a testament to the enduring legacy of its builder.
